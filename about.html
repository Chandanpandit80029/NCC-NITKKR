About National Cadet Corps

Learn about the history, vision, and structure of India's premier youth organization

Overview

The National Cadet Corps (NCC) is the largest youth organization in India. It was established under the National Cadet Corps Act of 1948 and has since grown to become one of the largest uniformed youth organizations in the world.

The NCC aims to develop character, comradeship, discipline, a secular outlook, and the ideals of selfless service among young citizens.

Our Motto

"Unity and Discipline"

Vision & Mission

Our Vision

To build a motivated and organized youth leadership that provides the nation with dedicated volunteers in all walks of life and always available for the service of the nation.

Our Mission

To develop character, comradeship, discipline, leadership, secular outlook, spirit of adventure, and ideals of selfless service amongst the youth of the country.

Our Values

Unity, Discipline, Patriotism, Secularism, Selfless Service, and Leadership are the core values that guide every NCC activity and program.

NCC Structure

Army Wing

Largest wing focusing on ground defense, leadership, and adventure activities.

  • Weapons Training
  • Map Reading
  • Field Craft & Battle Craft
  • Adventure Activities

Navy Wing

Specialized in naval training, sailing, and maritime awareness.

  • Seamanship
  • Navigation
  • Communication
  • Maritime Awareness

Air Force Wing

Focuses on aviation, aeromodelling, and air defense awareness.

  • Principles of Flight
  • Aero Modelling
  • Air Navigation
  • Meteorology

Role of NCC in Nation Building

Leadership Development

NCC provides opportunities for cadets to develop leadership qualities through various training programs, camps, and exercises.

Defense Training

Cadets receive basic military training that instills discipline, patriotism, and a sense of responsibility towards the nation.

Community Service

NCC cadets actively participate in social service activities, disaster management, and community development programs.
